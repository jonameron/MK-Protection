# protchain — Decentralized Cellphone Insurance

The first concept and prototype work for **protchain**, a decentralized cellphone-insurance
platform on Ethereum. Presented at D1Conf (Decentralized Insurance Conference), Prague, on
29 October 2018.

- Talk: https://youtu.be/aDQXEdarVkI
- Slides: [protchain-d1conf-prague-2018.pdf](protchain-d1conf-prague-2018.pdf)
- Site (archived): http://www.protchain.org

## The thesis

Insurance is naturally a community activity, so it is possible to cut the middle man — the
insurance company — out of risk coverage within a community. Mobile insurance is a strong
first use case for three reasons:

- **The data is available.** Object-specific information (IMEI, broken-glass detection) is
  digitally available and can be parametrized.
- **The market is large and growing.** Mobile insurance was growing ~10%/yr, with a
  potential market size of $27bn by 2020.
- **It can be decentralized step by step.** Start with third-party risk carriers and claims
  regulators, and hand those roles to the community as the ecosystem matures.

## How it works

protchain runs the core insurance processes — underwriting, claims management, proof of
identity, proof of ownership — anchored on Ethereum:

- **Customer** provides information for proof points and an Ethereum address.
- **The protchain community** takes part in operational processes (policy acceptance,
  claims regulation, pricing), decides on the risk and business model, and carries risk.
- **Oracles** feed in phone metadata and community signals.

The roadmap from the 2018 talk: complete the prototype (customer UI, claims-handling UI,
external API for claims and risk carry-over), negotiate risk-carrier and claims-handling
partnerships, and grow the community.
